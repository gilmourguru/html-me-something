Fuzz

David's Fuzz:

Fuzz Face - (BC109 silicon)

Vol: 2-3:00 | Fuzz: 5:00

The Dallas Arbiter Fuzz Face® was the fuzz of choice for David Gilmour in the late 60s and early 70s. By the time Dark Side of the Moon was recorded in 1972, David was using a silicon transistor model of the fuzz unit.

GilmourGuru's Fuzz:

Analogman BC109 - Time Clock Sun Face

Vol: 2-2:30 | Fuzz: 5:00

The Analogman BC109 Floyd Custom Fuzz is handmade in the USA, and is using the same silicon transistors found in David's 1971 - 1975 Fuzz units used on DSOTM as well as the supporting tours.

Compression

David's Comp:

MXR Dyna Comp

Vol: 2:00 | Sustain: 10:00

Although in 1972 David didn't use a Compressor pedal, he has been known to use up to 3 compressor pedals in a live scenario to achieve his tone for the intro section of Time. I personally use a Dyna Comp styled pedal into a Boss® CS-2 for the Time intro.

GilmourGuru's Comp:

DIY Dyna Comp Clone

Vol: 2:00 | Sustain: 10:30

My homemade DIY compression pedal is based upon the circuit from a 1976 MXR Dyna Comp. This is used on all clean tones such as the intro to 'Time'.

Boost

David's Boost:

Colorsound Powerboost

Vol: 12:00 | Bass: 12:00 | Treble: 9:00

The boost pedal of choice for Mr. Gilmour in the 70s was the Colorsound Powerboost. This was a silicon transistor booster with active bass & treble. David placed this after his Fuzz to boost the already fuzzed out tone to help push his amp into slight overdrive.

Gain: 12:00 | Bass: 12:00 | Treble: 9:30 | Vol: 2:00

The Thr≈çbak Overdrive Boost is one of the best clones of the old Colorsound Powerboost. Thr≈çbak has added a couple nice features to this unit. The other upgrade on this version is the inclusion of a pregain stage with a dedicated footswitch.

Volume Pedal

David's Vol. Pedal:

DeArmond Volume Pedal

David's volume pedal of choice early on was the DeArmond Volume Pedal. In later years he would use Dunlop® wah housings modified to act as volume & tone pedals. The most important thing about the placement of the volume pedal is that it is placed in front of your delay pedal so as to not cut off the trails of your delay.

GilmourGuru's Vol. Pedal:

Ernie Ball Volume Pedal Jr.

This is the 250K model. I don't use the tuner out.

Echo

David's Echo:

Binson Echorec 2

The Floyd's echo of choice until the mid - late 70s was the Binson Echorec 2. This delay used a magnetic rotating drum with 4 separte record + playback heads that produced a slightly modulated echo. The track 'Time' features 'swell' mode. The unit also has echo & repeat modes.

GilmourGuru's Echo:

Catalinbread Echorec

Mix: 10:00-12:00 | Swell: 12:00 | Tone: 11:15 | Time: 10:00 (310 ms PG 1) | Program (Opt 1): 1 (repeat: head 4) | Program (Opt 2): 12 (swell)

The Catalinbread Echorec offers 12 modes of operation combining the 4 different delay heads in various combinations including swell mode which David used for Time. Unlike the original Binson model as well as many current Echorec inspired clones, the Catalinbread Echorec delivers over 310 ms of total delay time, up to 2000 ms depending upon which head mode you're using.

Modulation / Rotary

David's Rotary:

Leslie® 147

Although, technically a speaker cabinet, most people aren't lucky enough to have their own rotating speaker, so this is listed as a modulation effect. For DSOTM David used Leslie® cabinets as well as a Maestro Rover. David usually blended his rotary cabinets lower in volume than his dry Hiwatt® cabs.

GilmourGuru's Rotary:

Yamaha® RA-200

I use a Fender Twin Reverb® preamp in front of the Yamaha. The 3 rotating speakers give ample options for mic placement allowing easy adjustment of the tremolo or chorus effects of the rotary speakers.
