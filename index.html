Fuzz

The Dallas Arbiter Fuzz Face® was the fuzz of choice for David Gilmour in the late 60s and early 70s. By the time Dark Side of the Moon was recorded in 1972, David was using a silicon transistor model of the fuzz unit.

Compression

Although in 1972 David didn't use a Compressor pedal, he has been known to use up to 3 compressor pedals in a live scenario to achieve his tone for the intro section of Time. I personally use a Dyna Comp styled pedal into a Boss® CS-2 for the Time intro.

Boost

The boost pedal of choice for Mr. Gilmour in the 70s was the Colorsound Powerboost. This was a silicon transistor booster with active bass & treble. David placed this after his Fuzz to boost the already fuzzed out tone to help push his amp into slight overdrive.

Volume Pedal

David's volume pedal of choice early on was the DeArmond Volume Pedal. In later years he would use Dunlop® wah housings modified to act as volume & tone pedals. The most !important thing about the placement of the volume pedal is that it is placed in front of your delay pedal so as to not cut off the trails of your delay.

Echo

The Floyd's echo of choice until the mid - late 70s was the Binson Echorec 2. This delay used a magnetic rotating drum with 4 separte record + playback heads that produced a slightly modulated echo. The track 'Time' features 'swell' mode. The unit also has echo & repeat modes.

Modulation

Although, technically a speaker cabinet, most people aren't lucky enough to have their own rotating speaker, so this is listed as a modulation effect. For DSOTM David used Leslie® cabinets as well as a Maestro Rover. David usually blended his rotary cabinets lower in volume than his dry Hiwatt® cabs.
